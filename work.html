Project Overview

A fashion startup empowering users to build personalized wardrobes and exchange pre-loved clothing, I led the end-to-end design of the website - focusing on the landing, settings, and profile pages to create an intuitive, user-centered experience.

Pain Point

On average, people own 148 pieces of clothing, yet still feel they have “nothing to wear.” Our goal is to turn this problem into personalized outfit choices that boost brand visibility and sales.

Style Explorer

Secondhand shoppers often compare price, condition, and brand reputation before deciding to buy.

Clear product photos help buyers understand fit, texture, color, and item quality.

Younger fashion shoppers are drawn to resale because it feels affordable, expressive, and sustainable.

Trust becomes a key barrier when users cannot verify sellers or item condition.

Buyers want size, material, and condition details before committing to a pre-loved item.

A personalized wardrobe experience can make browsing feel more curated and less overwhelming.

Users often need reassurance around payment, returns, and seller reliability.

Discovery works better when listings match personal style, budget, and size preferences.

Many users browse resale casually before they are ready to purchase.

High-quality listings reduce hesitation and make secondhand fashion feel more premium.

Sellers need simple tools to upload items, describe condition, and manage buyer questions.

Style inspiration can help users imagine how a secondhand item fits into their wardrobe.

Competitive platforms often rely on social proof, seller ratings, and visible transaction history.

A clean profile page can turn selling history into a signal of trust.

Users want to filter by brand, size, condition, color, price, and style category quickly.

Resale fashion is also identity, taste, and self-presentation.

Confusing settings make users less confident about privacy, notifications, and account control.

A smoother onboarding flow can help new users understand what the platform offers.

Saved items, liked products, and browsing history support future purchase decisions.

Fashion resale platforms need to balance playful browsing with transactional clarity.

Users are more likely to engage when recommendations feel personal rather than generic.

A strong landing page should communicate value, trust, and product personality quickly.

Price transparency helps buyers understand whether a resale item is a good deal.

Some shoppers hesitate because they worry the item may look different in person.

Community features can make resale feel less transactional and more like style discovery.

A consistent visual system helps the marketplace feel more trustworthy and polished.

Product cards should make key information scannable without overwhelming the user.

Sustainability messaging works best when it feels practical, not preachy.

The best resale experience helps users move from browsing to confident action.

A secondhand fashion platform needs both emotional appeal and operational trust.
